Jerod Wurm - CFP®, CRPC®, CMFC®

Partner, Financial Advisor

Jerod Wurm, CERTIFIED FINANCIAL PLANNER™ professional, has been serving the Greater Sacramento area as a financial advisor since 1997. He focuses in helping clients meet their financial goals through investment planning and management. Jerod says, "I'm always here for our clients. I keep an eye on their portfolios, and I stay in touch. Most important, I take the time to understand how people feel about their money and their financial security."

Through his comprehensive wealth management approach, Jerod assists his clients with pre-retirement planning, post retirement income strategies, investment portfolio management, long term care and estate planning strategies. He often works in conjunction with his client's existing tax and estate planning professionals.

Jerod has earned both the Chartered Retirement Planning CounselorSM (CRPC®) and Chartered Mutual Fund CounselorSM (CMFC®) professional designations from the College for Financial Planning. He is a member of the Northern California Financial Planning Association (NCFPA) and formerly served on the Board of Directors for the NCFPA. Supporting several charitable organizations, Jerod currently serves on the American Cancer Society's Partnership Council.

Jerod and his wife Sherril were high school sweethearts and grew up locally in Amador County. They have three children and reside in El Dorado Hills. Jerod can often be found on the field or on the courts coaching his children's athletic teams.

Why work with CERTIFIED FINANCIAL PLANNER® professionals?

When you work with CERTIFIED FINANCIAL PLANNER® professionals, you work with advisors who follow a strict Code of Ethics, Rules of Conduct, and Practice Standards. One of the key benefits for you is that CFP® professionals are required to hold themselves to the “Fiduciary Standard” and must at all times place the interest of the client first. Additionally, CFP® professionals remain current with changes in the financial planning field because they must pass a comprehensive financial planning examination and complete a minimum of 30 hours of continuing education every two years.
